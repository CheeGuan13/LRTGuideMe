Petaling Street

About Petaling Street

Petaling Street, also known as Chinatown Kuala Lumpur, is one of the city’s most iconic and historic districts. It is a lively marketplace filled with countless stalls selling everything from clothes, accessories, and souvenirs to traditional Chinese herbs and street food. Visitors can enjoy bargaining with vendors while experiencing the vibrant atmosphere that reflects the multicultural spirit of Kuala Lumpur.

At night, the area transforms into a colorful bazaar, with glowing lanterns lighting up the streets and food stalls offering local delicacies such as wantan mee, claypot chicken rice, and famous herbal teas. Beyond shopping and dining, the area is also home to several temples and heritage buildings that showcase the historical importance of the Chinese community in Malaysia.

Nearest LRT Station: Pasar Seni Station (KJ14)

Pasar Seni Station Information

  • Line: Kelana Jaya Line (KJ14), Sri Petaling Line (SP02)
  • Station Code: KJ14 / SP02
  • Interchange: Yes (2 lines)
  • Nearby Landmarks: Central Market, Petaling Street (Chinatown), River of Life, KL Bird Park, Islamic Arts Museum
  • Facilities: Elevator, Toilet, Customer Service Counter, Ticket Vending Machines

The station itself is designed for accessibility and comfort. It features escalators, elevators, and clear signages to guide travelers smoothly. Its underground layout also provides shelter from weather conditions, making it ideal for visitors who plan to explore the city on foot. Nearby facilities include food courts, convenience stores, and cultural spaces, ensuring that passengers have everything they need as soon as they step out.
